Research

At the University of Michigan–Flint, I participated in a research initiative aimed at adapting ride-on cars to better serve children with disabilities. The project was an interdisciplinary effort, bringing together engineering students, occupational therapists, and physical therapists to design modifications that addressed both functional needs and therapeutic goals. Through this collaboration, I gained experience working in large, diverse teams with different areas of expertise and perspectives. Our work emphasized not only creating tailored solutions for individual children but also developing standardized approaches that could be applied more broadly, ensuring consistency, accessibility, and long-term impact for future adaptations.
